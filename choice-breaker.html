Method 1: The Dread vs. Relief Duel

The tasks we avoid most are usually "Monsters" causing a massive, background drain on our mental RAM. Choosing to tackle even a tiny piece of this task provides the single highest emotional ROI because it immediately stops the background anxiety.

The 3-Step Choice Protocol:

  1. Scan your list and isolate the **one task** you are actively avoiding or feeling guilty about.
  2. Write down the absolute smallest, most ridiculously easy micro-step for it (e.g., "Open the document and write the title").
  3. Set the 60-second decision timer below. Commit *only* to doing that micro-step. Once the dread is broken, momentum takes over.

Method 2: The Domino Selector

Instead of looking at what is "important," look for the **Lever**. This is the task that, once completed, makes other tasks on your list easier, faster, or completely irrelevant.

The 3-Step Choice Protocol:

  1. Look at your list and ask: "If I could only do ONE thing today that would make tomorrow 50% easier, what is it?"
  2. Look for tasks that unblock someone else, provide crucial information you need for other tasks, or clear a major bottleneck.
  3. If you find that "Domino" task, choose it immediately. Set the 60-second timer to lock in your decision.

Method 3: The Battery Match Filter

Standard prioritization ignores your biological battery. Forcing a high-focus task when your dopamine is depleted leads to a shame spiral. You must match your choice to your current brain state.

Level 3 (High Battery)

Creative, problem-solving, deep focus, heavy writing.

Level 2 (Medium Battery)

Routine tasks, responding to standard emails, editing.

Level 1 (Low Battery)

Physical tasks, filing, cleaning, mindless data entry.

The 3-Step Choice Protocol:

  1. Check your physical energy. Rate it: Level 3, Level 2, or Level 1.
  2. Filter your list to *only* show tasks that match your current level.
  3. Pick any task from that matched list. If you are at Level 1, do not even look at Level 3 tasks.

Method 4: The Binary Bracket

The ADHD brain cannot compare 10 things at once; working memory bottlenecks. But it *can* easily compare 2 things. This method uses a tournament-style bracket to eliminate choice.

The 3-Step Choice Protocol:

  1. Pick any **two** tasks from your list. Ask: "Between these two, which one is more urgent or satisfying?"
  2. Eliminate the loser. Take the winner and compare it to a *third* task.
  3. Repeat this binary "duel" 3 or 4 times. The final standing task is your immediate priority. (Try our interactive version in the next tab!)

## 🧠 ADHD Choice-Breaker Flowchart

> **The Golden Rule:** The ADHD brain is paralyzed by too many options. Bypass the prefrontal cortex by forcing binary decisions or matching tasks to physical energy.

### ⚡ Quick Diagnostic Flowchart
1. **Why are you stuck choosing right now?**
   * **"One heavy task is making me avoid everything."** ➔ Go to **Method 1: Dread vs. Relief Duel**
   * **"I want to make sure I get the best ROI."** ➔ Go to **Method 2: Domino Selector**
   * **"My energy is fluctuating wildly."** ➔ Go to **Method 3: Battery Match Filter**
   * **"I have too many options and they all look equal."** ➔ Go to **Method 4: Binary Bracket** or **Method 5: 60-Second Coin Flip**

---

### 🛠️ The 5-Minute Choice Protocols

#### 1. The Dread vs. Relief Duel (Risk Reduction)
* **Goal:** Break cognitive dread and stop background anxiety.
* **Protocol:**
  1. Scan your list and isolate the ONE task causing a background hum of guilt/anxiety.
  2. Write down the absolute smallest, most ridiculously easy micro-step for it.
  3. Commit *only* to doing that micro-step. Once the dread is broken, momentum takes over.

#### 2. The Domino Selector (High ROI / Leverage)
* **Goal:** Find the task that unblocks others or clears a major bottleneck.
* **Protocol:**
  1. Ask: *"If I could only do ONE thing today that would make tomorrow 50% easier, what is it?"*
  2. Look for tasks that unblock someone else, provide crucial information, or clear a major bottleneck.
  3. If you find that "Domino" task, choose it immediately.

#### 3. The Battery Match Filter (Momentum Preservation)
* **Goal:** Match your choice to your biological battery.
* **Protocol:**
  * **Level 3 (High Battery):** Creative, problem-solving, deep focus, heavy writing.
  * **Level 2 (Medium Battery):** Routine tasks, responding to standard emails, editing.
  * **Level 1 (Low Battery):** Physical tasks, filing, cleaning, mindless data entry.
  * *Action:* Check your energy level, filter out mismatched tasks, and choose from what remains.

#### 4. The Binary Bracket (Working Memory Offloader)
* **Goal:** Eliminate choice overload by comparing exactly two options at a time.
* **Protocol:**
  1. Pick any TWO tasks. Ask: *"Which is more urgent or satisfying?"*
  2. Eliminate the loser. Pit the winner against a third task.
  3. Repeat until only one task remains.

#### 5. The 60-Second Coin Flip (The Gut-Check)
* **Goal:** Bypass the prefrontal cortex and tap into your subconscious desire.
* **Protocol:**
  1. Pick your top 2 competing tasks. Assign one to Heads, one to Tails.
  2. Flip a coin.
  3. The moment the coin lands, notice your gut reaction. If you feel a flash of disappointment or relief, ignore the coin and go with what your gut actually wanted.
