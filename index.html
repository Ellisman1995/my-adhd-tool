Your Prescription: The Dopamine Ramp

Your executive function is temporarily locked. Trying to do your "most important" task right now is a trap. We need to generate easy, friction-free dopamine first, then hijack that momentum.

The 3-Minute Protocol:

  1. Look at your workspace or list. Pick the absolute easiest, most satisfying, or most novel task.
  2. It must take under 5 minutes (e.g., throw away trash, clear 3 emails, tidy your immediate desk space).
  3. Set the timer below for 3 minutes and race to complete it before the beep.

Your Prescription: Relief-First & Micro-Slicing

You are experiencing cognitive dread. A single "Monster" task is draining your mental RAM. We are going to shrink this monster down until it no longer triggers your nervous system's fight-or-flight response.

The Monster:

It must be so small that it feels stupid to write down (e.g., "Open Google Doc and write title").

Your Only Mission Right Now:

Do not think about finishing the whole task. Just do this tiny step. Once done, momentum will take over.

Your Prescription: Fire vs. Lever Duel

When everything is shouting for attention, your brain treats a minor email and a major deadline with the same level of panic. We need to force a head-to-head comparison to separate real risk from future value.

The Fire (Risk Reduction)

What is the ONE task that, if not done in the next few hours, will result in immediate negative consequences?

The Lever (Future ROI)

What is the ONE task that, if completed, will make 3 other tasks easier or completely irrelevant tomorrow?

The 3-Minute Protocol:

  1. Identify your one Fire and your one Lever.
  2. Ask yourself: "Am I currently fueled by urgency (Fire), or do I have the brainpower to invest in the future (Lever)?"
  3. Pick one, cross out the other for now, and start. Set a 3-minute timer to initiate action.

Your Prescription: Energy-State Match

Forcing an ADHD brain to do high-focus analytical work when it is in a low-dopamine, fatigued state is like trying to start a car with no gas. Let's match your task to your actual biological battery.

Level 3 (High Battery)

Creative, problem-solving, high focus, heavy writing.

Level 2 (Medium Battery)

Routine tasks, responding to standard emails, editing.

Level 1 (Low Battery)

Physical tasks, filing, cleaning, mindless data entry.

The 3-Minute Protocol:

  1. Acknowledge your current level. If you are at Level 1, do not touch Level 3 tasks.
  2. Find a Level 1 task (e.g., shredding papers, organizing folders, deleting spam).
  3. Doing low-energy tasks keeps momentum alive, whereas forcing high-energy tasks leads to a shame spiral.

## 🧠 ADHD Emergency Prioritization Flowchart

> **The Golden Rule:** An ADHD brain in motion stays in motion. Prioritize for *momentum*, not just importance.

### ⚡ Quick Diagnostic Flowchart
1. **How does your brain feel right now?**
   * **Paralyzed / Staring Blankly** ➔ Go to **Method 2: Dopamine Ramp**
   * **Anxious / Looming Dread** ➔ Go to **Method 1: Relief-First Filter** or **Method 5: Micro-Slicing**
   * **Overwhelmed by Urgent Things** ➔ Go to **Method 3: Fire vs. Lever Duel**
   * **Exhausted / Low Battery** ➔ Go to **Method 4: Energy-State Match**

---

### 🛠️ The 5-Minute Emergency Protocols

#### 1. Relief-First Filter (Cognitive Decongestion)
* **Goal:** Remove the task draining your mental RAM.
* **Protocol:**
  1. Scan your list quickly.
  2. Identify the ONE task causing a background hum of guilt/anxiety.
  3. Circle it and hide the rest of the list.
  4. Commit to working on it for just 3 minutes.

#### 2. The Dopamine Ramp (Momentum Builder)
* **Goal:** Lower the barrier to entry to break paralysis.
* **Protocol:**
  1. Pick an absolute easiest, most satisfying task (takes < 5 minutes).
  2. Set a timer for 3 minutes.
  3. Complete it immediately, then use that dopamine spike to start a harder task.

#### 3. Fire vs. Lever Duel (Urgency Filter)
* **Goal:** Separate real risk from future value.
* **Protocol:**
  1. Pick **one Fire** (immediate negative consequences if ignored).
  2. Pick **one Lever** (makes 3 other tasks easier tomorrow).
  3. Ask: *"Am I fueled by urgency (Fire) or do I have brainpower for future ROI (Lever)?"*
  4. Choose one, cross out the other, and start.

#### 4. Energy-State Match (Energy Preservation)
* **Goal:** Work with your biology, not against it.
* **Protocol:**
  * **Level 3 (High Battery):** Creative, problem-solving, deep focus.
  * **Level 2 (Medium Battery):** Routine tasks, responding to emails, editing.
  * **Level 1 (Low Battery):** Physical tasks, filing, cleaning, mindless data entry.
  * *Action:* Tag tasks 1-3 and match your current battery level.

#### 5. The Micro-Slicing Monster Tamer (Anxiety Reducer)
* **Goal:** Shrink the task until it no longer triggers fight-or-flight.
* **Protocol:**
  1. Write down the giant "Monster" task you are avoiding.
  2. Write down the absolute smallest, easiest first step (takes < 1 minute).
  3. Commit *only* to doing that micro-step.
